Sara Maleubre

Hello! I am a graduate student at Sorbonne Universiy, France, working with Michael Joyce. I'm interested in the formation of large scale structures in the Universe, which I explore by analysing results from numerical simulations. My current research focuses on the accuracy at which we can retrieve information from these numerical approximations of our Universe. With this purpose, I run and analyse N-body simulations to better understand their discretization limits, and how they affect the computed statistics. Have a look at my research for a more formal description of my latest work on this an other exciting topics.

N-Body simulations

In order to study the characteristics of our Universe, researchers use computer simulations that reproduce, to an approximation, what we can see through observations. They aim at answering some of the most pressing mysteries in cosmology, such as the nature of dark matter and dark energy.

Modified Gravity

Our universe is currently expanding, and we are not exactly sure of the source driving this acceleration. Different explanations have been postulated: a cosmological constant, an aditional anti-gravity energy source called Dark Energy, or simply general relativity is incompleted and the correct modifications would solve the problem.

About

Growing up in Spain, I've moved around to different european countries for research and studies. I did my undergraduate for the most part at Universidad Autonoma de Madrid, Spain, where I specialized in Astronomy. I then moved to Leiden University, The Netherlands, for a master in Astronomy and Cosmology. I'm now a graduate student at Sorbonne University, France, but currently based at the Max Planck Institute for extraterrestrial phyisics, Germany.
